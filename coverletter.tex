\begin{cvletter}

Studying Computer Science changed the way I understand technology and its role in the world. What began as curiosity about how computers work became a deeper appreciation for what they make possible: precision, consistency, automation, pattern recognition... list goes on.

That interest eventually matured into a more practical conviction, technology's value does not lie in its sophistication but in the problems it solves and the improvements it creates in people's lives. That realization led me to product management.

In product roles I learned to work at the intersection of strategy, operations and technology. Conversations with leadership sharpened my understanding of long-term goals and organizational priorities. Engagement with employees on the ground revealed the operational inefficiencies and friction points that often remain invisible at a distance. Bridging these perspectives taught me meaningful innovation begins with a clear understanding of the problem. After the "what do we want to solve" has been defined does technology come in to answer "how do we solve it".

This way of thinking shaped my professional direction. I became increasingly motivated by the challenge of designing solutions that improve efficiency, strengthen decision-making and create measurable value for organizations, the people within them and their customers.

In recent years, that motivation has become even stronger with the rapid development of artificial intelligence. What first appeared to be nothing more than smart chatbots has become a wave that will likely transform every aspect of society. Yet the central challenge remains: not simply adopting new technologies but truly understanding them so as to know which problems they're best suited to solve.

I currently find myself in the awkward position of not truly understanding this new technology which means I'm no longer able to craft the best possible solutions to the problems I come across. Hence my application, this course will equip me with the knowledge and skills needed to navigate this new landscape.

Not only is the course content aligned with my education and work experience but it's also geared towards the AI dominated world we're marching towards.

I view this program as more than an academic milestone, it is a deliberate step toward the kind of impact I want to make. I believe NOVA IMS will offer the academic rigor, real-world focus and collaborative environment that will help me develop into a professional capable of navigating this new world.

\end{cvletter}
