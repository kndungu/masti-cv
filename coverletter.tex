\lettersection{How it started}
In the second year of my Biochemistry undergraduate studies, MIT and Harvard partnered to launch edx; an online platform through which anyone could take some of their courses for free. I was obsessed with MIT growing up and jumped at the opportunity to take one of their courses, just to see if I could. That course was 6.00x: Introduction to Computer Science and Programming and it was one of the hardest things I had ever done, I was instantly hooked. Afterwards I took Harvard's version of the same course and later multiple programming courses on edx, Coursera, Udacity and Udemy.

\lettersection{Early Career}
While in my final year of university Andela launched in Kenya with a unique proposition, they'd teach you how to code then place you in jobs at top US companies. It was too good an opportunity to pass up. At Andela I found structure, I was no longer learning anything and everything that piqued my interest but rather the most in demand skills, industry best practices, and it gave me an opportunity to practice my soft skills. Though my first placement was short lived, I got valuable experience that was instrumental in my success at future placements.

\lettersection{Finding a rhythm}
Eventually I got to work with some truly exceptional managers and teammates who provided not only a challenging environment that promoted growth, but also a safe space where one could fail and learn. Here my skills and responsibilities grew until I became a key member of the team.

\lettersection{What I offer}
I'm now ready to move on to my next challenge. I'm particularly excited about the possibility of working with DataCamp because I believe having skills in Data Science and AI will become a basic competency that everyone will soon need. I'd bring with me all the knowledge and skills I've acquired in the last seven years of working in software development together with a genuine curiosity and willingness to learn more. I'm product focused and believe writing code is merely a means to end, what's most important is being able to work with the entire team to deliver a product that truly delights customers.

\end{cvletter}
